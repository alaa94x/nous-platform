# Nous Creative Direction Research

Date: 13 July 2026  
Expanded: 13 July 2026 — cross-category research beyond award and agency websites  
Scope: brand expression, typography, color, copy, desktop/mobile composition, interaction, motion, accessibility, and admin content implications

## Executive decision

Keep the supplied palette. Replace the visual grammar around it.

The three greens are a strong fit for Nous: they feel technical without using the predictable blue/purple AI palette, and they can move between premium, natural, and computational. They are already present in the codebase at the supplied values. The problem is not the colors; it is that the current composition combines several familiar award-site signals at once:

- oversized editorial serif;
- tiny, widely tracked mono labels;
- technical grid and orbit diagrams;
- bracketed section labels;
- dark “control-room” framing;
- generic agency phrases such as “Engineered Intelligence,” “Our DNA,” and “Selected Work.”

Each choice is defensible on its own. Together they make the site feel designed from references rather than from Nous.

The recommended direction is **Living Logic**: a calm, high-contrast visual system in which the Nous loop becomes a responsive field, content is set in a confident contemporary sans, proof is given more space than decoration, and motion shows ideas resolving into working systems. It should feel intelligent, Gulf-aware, and alive without behaving like a portfolio experiment.

## What the research says

[CSS Design Awards’ 2025 Website of the Year results](https://www.cssdesignawards.com/blog/2025-website-of-the-year-winners/430/) are useful because the winners split across UX, UI, innovation, portfolio, and agency categories. Dropbox Brand by Daybreak won overall and Best UX; Exat Typeface won Best UI; Bruno Simon won Best Portfolio; Bürocratik won Best Agency. The lesson is not “add more WebGL.” The strongest work gives one recognizable idea a complete system and lets the content determine the interaction.

The live review also exposed what not to copy:

- [Unseen Studio](https://unseen.co/) can support an entrance screen and sound choice because immersion is the product. Nous needs immediate clarity and should not gate the homepage.
- [Lusion](https://lusion.co/) can lead with heavy real-time 3D because that is its core service. Nous should use one efficient signature system, not make every visitor wait for a demo reel.
- [Bürocratik](https://www.burocratik.com/) succeeds through a very ownable voice, bold art direction, and obsessive detail—not through one reusable layout formula.
- [Exo Ape](https://www.exoape.com/) proves that mobile is strongest when it is recomposed, not when desktop is uniformly scaled down.
- [Locomotive’s Drake Hotel case study](https://locomotive.ca/en/work/the-drake-hotel) treats the website as a cultural publication because that fits the client. The content model creates the design.
- [Daybreak’s Dropbox work](https://www.daybreak.studio/work/dropbox) is a useful model for a broad brand system: expressive, but structured enough to scale.

### What changed after expanding the search

The original Living Logic recommendation remains, but it is now more specific:

1. **The Nous Field cannot be a decorative fluid or particle background.** It must derive from the two-part Nous mark and react to actual content states.
2. **The field needs a useful child system called Nous Instruments.** Service maps, project timelines, process paths, metrics, filters, and form progress are all functional expressions of the same logic.
3. **Authentic evidence must carry more visual weight.** Real screens, diagrams, decisions, outcomes, team process, and client material should interrupt the generative system.
4. **Arabic becomes a design input at the start.** Its line shape, cadence, direction, and type proportions influence the grid and motion origins.
5. **The light theme becomes more important.** Product, archive, and cultural references show that a recognizable technical brand does not need a permanently dark “control room.” Dark and light chapters will make the green palette richer and less template-like.
6. **The mobile version must be authored first for each signature moment.** It gets fewer objects but equal conceptual quality.

### Template saturation audit

The current Framer, Webflow, Readymag, and Spline ecosystems repeatedly use:

- black background plus glowing blur;
- oversized high-contrast serif next to a neutral sans;
- rounded project cards floating over a gradient;
- cursor masks, magnetic buttons, and liquid pointer effects;
- generic 3D chrome objects;
- “award-winning creative studio” copy;
- smooth-scroll reveals applied to every element;
- horizontal portfolio reels that work poorly without a mouse.

These patterns are not automatically bad. They are simply too available to form the center of Nous. The plan now treats resemblance to premium templates as a rejection criterion during design review.

### Cross-category conclusion

The most memorable examples do three things simultaneously:

- they can be recognized from a cropped fragment;
- their interaction reflects the content or product;
- they preserve clarity when motion, images, or custom fonts are removed.

That becomes the standard for Nous. A still frame should look like Nous; a moving frame should behave like Nous; and the semantic page should still explain Nous.

## Palette verdict

### Keep these anchors

| Token | Value | Recommended role |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Emerald Pine | `#084734` | Structural panels, active navigation, large chapter moments, illustration depth |
| Lime Glow | `#CEF17B` | Primary CTA, focus, selected state, key data, short moments of energy |
| Green Tea | `#CDEDB3` | Calm secondary panels, diagrams, large tint areas, soft highlights |

All tested core pairs are strong:

| Pair | Contrast |
| --- | ---: |
| Lime Glow on `#111D1A` | 13.58:1 |
| Green Tea on `#111D1A` | 13.47:1 |
| Current muted `#8AADA3` on `#111D1A` | 7.07:1 |
| Emerald Pine on Lime Glow | 8.41:1 |
| Emerald Pine on Green Tea | 8.34:1 |
| Current off-white `#F0EDEA` on `#111D1A` | 14.83:1 |

These comfortably exceed the [WCAG minimum contrast thresholds](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/contrast-minimum.html). The current readability problem is therefore mostly size, weight, tracking, and density—not the core color pairings.

### Complete the palette

Three brand colors are not a full interface system. Add a controlled neutral and semantic ramp:

```css
--ink-950: #07110E;       /* hero / deepest canvas */
--ink-900: #111D1A;       /* default dark canvas */
--pine-900: #084734;      /* supplied Emerald Pine */
--pine-700: #12634C;      /* hover / data variation */
--lime-400: #CEF17B;      /* supplied Lime Glow */
--tea-200: #CDEDB3;       /* supplied Green Tea */
--paper-50: #F3F0E9;      /* warm light canvas */
--mist-400: #8AADA3;      /* secondary copy on dark */
--line-dark: rgba(205, 237, 179, .16);
--line-light: rgba(8, 71, 52, .18);
--error: #FF807A;
--warning: #F4C95D;
--info: #79BFE8;
```

Suggested visual distribution:

- 60–65% ink and dark green canvases;
- 15–20% warm light or Green Tea breathing space;
- 10–15% Emerald Pine structure;
- no more than 5–8% Lime Glow in a typical viewport.

Lime loses its premium quality when it fills too many panels. Use it as energy, not wallpaper.

## Typography verdict

The current Fraunces + Space Mono + IBM Plex Sans Arabic combination is attractive, but it is also a recognizable “editorial technology” recipe. Fraunces makes the hero elegant; Space Mono makes it technical. Used this extensively, they push Nous toward the same visual category as many agency templates.

### Recommended system

**Practical free/open-source direction**

- Latin display and body: `Instrument Sans Variable`.
- Arabic display: `Alexandria Variable` or a carefully tested Arabic companion with similar width and weight.
- Arabic long-form body: keep `IBM Plex Sans Arabic` if it wins the readability test.
- Instrument labels only: `Space Mono`, reduced to metadata, numbers, and code-like states.

**Premium direction if a font license is approved**

- Commission or license one characterful grotesk with Latin and Arabic support.
- Use one family across both languages where possible; this is the strongest way to make bilingual layouts feel like one brand.

Fraunces can remain for an occasional quotation or case-study editorial moment, but it should stop being the main identity.

### Type scale

| Role | Desktop | Mobile | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Hero display | `clamp(72px, 9vw, 148px)` | `clamp(46px, 14vw, 68px)` | Tighter Latin leading; Arabic tested independently |
| Section display | `clamp(44px, 5.5vw, 88px)` | `36–52px` | Fewer forced line breaks |
| Lead paragraph | `20–26px` | `18–21px` | 50–68 characters per line |
| Body | `17–19px` | `16–18px` | Default readable copy |
| Metadata | `12–13px` | `12px` minimum | Mono only here; moderate tracking |
| Button | `14–16px` | `15–16px` | Minimum 48px physical height |

The current 8–11px labels and paragraph text should be removed from meaningful content. Strong contrast does not make very small text comfortable.

## Direction, bilingual layout, and rhythm

- Treat English and Arabic as parallel compositions, not mirrored screenshots.
- Use logical CSS properties and true reading-order changes.
- Let Arabic headings take more horizontal space and slightly more line height.
- Avoid forced uppercase styling as the main hierarchy; Arabic has no equivalent.
- Do not place Arabic in a decorative supporting role beneath English. Each locale should feel primary on its route.
- Use a consistent 12-column desktop grid, but let media and fields break it intentionally.
- Use a 4-column mobile grid with 20px gutters and no edge-to-edge body text.
- Alternate dense evidence sections with quiet color fields so the site has rhythm beyond “dark section plus divider.”

## Recommended creative direction: Living Logic

### Core idea

Nous does not merely add intelligence to interfaces. It turns ambiguity into understandable systems. The visual behavior should repeatedly move from many possibilities to one clear structure.

### Signature visual

Replace the generic orbit/grid combination with a **Nous Field** and a family of **Nous Instruments**:

- the loop mark defines two interacting attractors;
- fine paths, particles, or typographic fragments continuously reorganize between them;
- the field has autonomous idle motion, so it never freezes when the mouse stops;
- pointer, touch, scroll velocity, and optionally device tilt influence it without being required;
- transitions resolve into diagrams, project media, or section dividers as the visitor moves down the page;
- mobile uses a deliberately simpler field with fewer particles and a lower frame budget.

The field should not resemble a liquid cursor toy. It should look like reasoning becoming structure.

Nous Instruments turn the field into useful interface behavior:

- **Lens:** focuses a complex service map around one outcome;
- **Trace:** shows the path from problem through decision to shipped result;
- **Assembly:** makes process steps lock into a working whole;
- **Pulse:** gives restrained feedback to quotes, metrics, or live states;
- **Convergence:** gathers paths into a decision or CTA;
- **Archive:** organizes real project media and notes without decorative distortion.

The visual rule is “reveal the mechanism.” Inspired by product brands such as Nothing and Teenage Engineering, small construction details—joins, states, labels, constraints, and outputs—should be visible when they help visitors understand the work.

### Motion grammar

| Motion layer | Timing | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Press / focus feedback | 120–180ms | Confirm input immediately |
| Hover / state change | 180–240ms | Explain affordance |
| Copy/media reveal | 500–800ms | Establish reading order |
| Section transition | 800–1200ms | Connect chapters |
| Ambient field | 12–24s cycles | Keep the system alive without demanding attention |

Rules:

- continuous motion must not depend on mouse movement;
- every hover behavior needs a touch/focus equivalent;
- animation should usually affect transform, opacity, clipping, or shader uniforms;
- pause or reduce expensive canvas work offscreen and when the page is hidden;
- honor reduced-motion with a static but still well-composed state;
- no universal parallax, bouncing cards, long preloaders, custom cursor dependency, or auto-advancing mobile carousel.

This follows the interaction principles in the Emil design-engineering skill: make motion interruptible, purposeful, and faster on repeated interactions.

## The connective tissue: one remarkable journey

The experience should not feel like eight impressive sections made by eight different teams. It needs one creative spine:

> **Many possibilities enter. One clear system leaves.**

Every chapter inherits the same visual ingredients—two attractors from the Nous mark, paths, fields, convergence, and precise typographic rhythm—but changes their behavior to match the content. This is how the site stays related while each section remains surprising.

### The visitor's emotional sequence

| Chapter | Visitor feeling | Signature event | Connection to the next chapter |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Hero | “This is alive.” | An autonomous field discovers the Nous loop and resolves around the promise | One path leaves the hero and becomes the next section's reading rule |
| Why Nous | “They see the problem clearly.” | The path connects three proof points only when their meaning is read | The three lines combine into three system pathways |
| Systems we build | “They can structure complexity.” | A living map reorganizes around the selected business outcome | The chosen path opens into a real project frame |
| Built in practice | “They have actually done it.” | Project media emerges from the same field, then takes over at full fidelity | A project timeline line becomes the process line |
| How we work | “I understand how we would work together.” | The line progresses through concrete outputs, not abstract phase labels | The completed line becomes the baseline of a client quote |
| Client evidence | “Other serious people trust them.” | Quotes create subtle waves in the field; proof changes its behavior, not just its text | The final wave settles into a calm question list |
| Before we begin | “They have anticipated my concerns.” | Opening an answer locally rearranges the field instead of moving the entire page theatrically | The last answer draws the paths toward one final point |
| Final CTA | “I know what to do.” | Every path converges into the Nous mark, then the CTA becomes the only lime object | The mark and footer return to a quiet idle state |

### Memorable-detail system

The “shock” should come from the completeness of the idea, not from noise:

- **Continuity:** a line, particle, word, or visual state visibly survives between adjacent sections.
- **Meaningful transformation:** the field behaves differently for proof, process, work, and questions.
- **Microcopy:** interface language sounds like Nous—direct, intelligent, and human—not like template labels.
- **Soundless tactility:** controls compress, paths respond, media catches up, and states settle without requiring audio.
- **Bilingual craft:** the field and transition origins respect LTR/RTL reading direction; Arabic is not an afterthought.
- **Return motifs:** something first seen abstractly in the hero returns later as a useful diagram, project frame, or progress cue.
- **Hidden depth:** optional small details reward attention—coordinates, project build notes, system states—but never hide required content.

### The wow budget

Use one major memorable event per section, one minor interaction per component family, and quiet space around both. If every object animates, nothing feels special.

- Major event: 1 per section, content-specific, visible without a mouse.
- Minor response: buttons, links, filters, accordion, cards, and form state.
- Ambient layer: one shared field, never a second competing background effect.
- Quiet layer: at least 40–50% of each viewport should remain visually calm enough to read.

### Transition choreography

- Do not fade every section in from zero; preserve at least one visual object across the boundary.
- The outgoing section should hand over state before the incoming section animates.
- Scroll reversals must reverse or settle naturally rather than replay a canned sequence.
- Direct navigation and reduced motion must still show the final composed state immediately.
- On mobile, use simpler handoffs—line continuation, color-field change, clipped media—rather than miniature desktop choreography.

This connected system is the main differentiator. Competitors can imitate a dark palette or a fluid background; it is much harder to imitate a narrative in which every interaction expresses the same brand thought.

## Homepage architecture

### 1. Hero — one clear promise

Desktop:

- calm header with logo, Work, Capabilities, About, language, and one CTA;
- hero copy left or lower-left, but not trapped inside a technical frame;
- Nous Field occupies the canvas and gently crosses behind the copy with protected contrast;
- one primary CTA, one quiet text link, and two honest proof markers;
- first project edge or next-section title visible below the fold.

Mobile:

- recompose into two acts instead of shrinking desktop;
- first 55–60%: promise, supporting line, CTA;
- lower 40–45%: simplified field that responds to touch and continues idling;
- `100svh` only when all content fits; allow natural height on short devices;
- no tiny “live system / ambient” labels;
- header target sizes at least 48px and a safe-area-aware persistent project CTA.

### 2. Why Nous — position before biography

- one short thesis;
- three proof points such as senior-led, Doha/GCC context, and strategy-to-shipping ownership;
- authentic team/process media when available;
- animated connector that resolves the three points into one engagement model.

### 3. Systems we build — outcomes before service names

- organize around business outcomes, not six equal service boxes;
- show 3 primary pathways: Launch, Modernize, Intelligence;
- reveal technical capabilities after the outcome is understood;
- desktop may use a responsive map; mobile uses a vertical list with one expanded item.

### 4. Built in practice — evidence is the visual hero

- larger, brighter project media;
- each card states problem, intervention, and outcome;
- motion comes from real interface/video detail, not generic card tilt;
- mobile uses one card per screen with an obvious next-card edge and no hover dependency.

### 5. How we work — show decisions moving

- four phases remain, but content is specific: Frame, Prototype, Build, Evolve;
- a path resolves as the visitor scrolls;
- each phase includes its output, not only an abstract description;
- mobile uses a vertical stepper with one active chapter and a persistent progress cue.

### 6. Client evidence

- lead with one strong, attributable quote;
- supporting quotes remain selectable, not auto-rotating;
- if meaningful metrics exist, connect each to its project source; never invent symmetry.

### 7. Before we begin

- accessible accordion;
- questions ordered by actual sales friction: fit, timeline, process, budget, support;
- one answer open on desktop; all closed or the first open on mobile.

### 8. Final CTA

- use a Green Tea or warm-paper field to break the dark rhythm;
- reuse the field as a calm convergence rather than another orbit;
- single line: “Bring us the hard problem.”
- show expected reply time and Doha location only if operationally true.

## Mobile quality rules

Mobile is a separate art direction pass, not a responsive cleanup.

- verify at 320, 360, 390, 430, and 768px;
- keep meaningful body text at 16px or larger;
- use at least 20px side gutters on phones;
- use 48px targets for primary controls, exceeding the [WCAG 2.2 minimum](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/target-size-minimum.html);
- keep the primary CTA above the safe area;
- place one dominant visual/message in each viewport, but never hide essential copy to force it;
- load a reduced visual asset set on mobile;
- cap the ambient canvas complexity and test low-power mode;
- ensure landscape mode does not trap the page beneath fixed navigation;
- test English and Arabic independently because line count and button width differ.

## Admin portal changes required with implementation

The public redesign and the admin model must ship together. Do not hard-code new public copy or motion choices into components.

### Global settings

- hero eyebrow, headline, deck, primary CTA label/link, secondary CTA label/link for EN/AR;
- proof-marker label/value pairs for EN/AR;
- final CTA heading, support line, and response-time claim for EN/AR;
- signature-field mode with safe options only: `calm`, `standard`, `off`;
- reduced-motion preview and mobile preview in admin.

### Section records

- localized public title and internal admin label;
- localized intro and accessible media alt text;
- section visibility and order;
- do not expose arbitrary colors, font sizes, or animation durations per section—the design system should remain controlled.

### Projects

- problem, intervention, outcome, and optional sourced metrics in EN/AR;
- hero media, mobile crop/media, detail media, poster image, and captions;
- media focal point and aspect-role rather than freeform pixel crops;
- optional accent selection limited to approved brand tokens;
- project completeness status for desktop, mobile, English, and Arabic.

### Services and process

- outcome group, short promise, full description, capabilities, and CTA in EN/AR;
- process phase output/deliverable in addition to title and description;
- translation completeness should block publication when required fields are missing.

## Alternative directions considered

### Gulf Modernism

Architectural spacing, bilingual typographic composition, material textures, and geometry influenced by regional light and built form. This can be highly ownable, but it risks becoming decorative “Middle East styling” if it is not grounded in real photography, language, and cultural collaboration.

### Evidence in Motion

A quieter, product-led site built almost entirely from case-study media, annotated interface detail, and measured outcomes. This is the most credible direction, but it requires a strong archive of authentic project visuals and metrics. It is an excellent layer within Living Logic rather than the entire identity today.
